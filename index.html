12 Ivana Franka street

Alex Maksiov, Ukraine

The chicadee symbolizes freedom that watches on people in the window throung the light bulb. Bulb lamp is playing the role of a thin barrier between humanity and freedom.

Protectress // Beregynya

1/2 Tarasa Shevchenka side street

Mata Ruda, Costa Rica

The mural was created in 2016 as a part of Art United Us project. It displays a portrait of a woman surrounded by sunflowers, traditional symbols of Ukraine. The mural plot is inspired by Slavic mythology and the character of beregynya, a Slavic goddess of housekeeping and house protection.

The Universal Language

Osokorky subway station

Apollo Torres, Brazil

The mural, which displays musicians speaking to their listeners the universal language of music, is the one of 8 drawings by 8 famious mural artists from all over the world that decorate the Osokorky subway station. The project was realised in 2018. The artist work every night from 1AM to 5AM while the subway was resting. Read more about the project.

The Ghost of Kyiv

3/7 Mezhygirska street

Andriy Kovtun, Ukraine

The Ghost of Kyiv is a composite character of air pilots that protected the sky of Kyiv during the first days after Russian invasion on February 24, 2022. The pilots destroyed 6 aircrafts during 30 hours and became the symbol of hope for many Kyivans. The mural is drawed with weather-resistant paints that are believed to last for about 10 years.

The Portrait of Sergey Nigoyan

22 Mykhailivska street

Alexandre Farto, Portugal

Sergey Nigoyan participated in the Revolution of Dignity in 2013-2014 and became the first protestant who was killed on Maydan. The work is also worth attention because of its unusual technuique. The portrait was made by removing the wall coating layer by layer, revealing the ancient brick texture of the wall.

Encumbering machine

Saksaganskogo street 145

Christian Rebecci & Pablo Togni aka NEVERCREW, Switzerland

The mural tells about unharmonious relations between humanity and nature that causes a severe damage to many animal spicies. The mural became the part of Art United Us project in 2017.

Ghost of Kyiv

36A Lesyi Ukrainky avenue

Guido van Helten , Australia

On the mural by famous Australian artist, a girl tries on Ukrainian traditional emproidered shirt vyshyvanka that symbolizes Ukrainian identity itself. The mural decorates 18-store building and takes 742 sq.m. The mural height is 42 meters that makes it probably the biggest vyshyvanka mural in the world.

The Dreamer

Striletska street 12

Fintan Magee, Australia

A jumping girl on the wall of residential building in 2015 is the famous Ukrainian gymnast Hanna Rizatdinova, who was born and raised in Crimea. The refion of Crimea was occupied by Russian Federation in February,2014. Hanna Rizatdinova achieved the bronze Olympic medal for Ukraine in 2016.
